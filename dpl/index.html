Definition and Purpose

What is a Digital Projects Librarian and what should s/he do?

Part librarian, part "IT person"

Digital

  • Anything represented using discrete values (vs. analog)
  • Not expressed in a physical medium

Projects

Implies something atypical, unique, or limited in scope; uncommon

Librarian

describe, classify, organize, regulate, protect, preserve, educate, empower

Archivists too!

What does s/he do?

  • A digital librarian is just a librarian in a different domain
  • Digital vs. analog or paper vs. pdf, the fundamentals are still the same
  • Everything is not digital ...yet?
  • Until then, one project at a time...

Challenges

What major challenges might you foresee with the work?

Preparing for potential changes while dealing the actual ones

The Information Age

  • Still in its infancy
  • Standards and practices are in flux
  • Data itself is fragile
  • It needs to grow up before we can properly deal with it

Sound familiar?

Europe, c.1440

  • Gutenberg's printing press
  • Beginning of the incunabula
  • Ends "officially" on January 1st, 1501
  • Continues as late as 1540

Midnight, December 31, 1969

  • Beginning of the digital incunabula
  • It ain't over until... 2030? 2050? 2070?

Technology

The cause of (and solution to) all our problems

  • raises expectations
  • drives changes in standards
  • triggers obsolescence
  • moves waaaay too fast

Is there any good news?

  • We're all in this together
  • Standards are built through collaboration
  • Openness protects you from obsolescence

Programming Strategies
